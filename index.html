Moisture & Leaks

Water staining and roof sheathingstained deckingrusted nails

Look For

  • Water stains on sheathing or rafters
  • Damp, soft, or spongy wood
  • Moldy odor or visible growth
  • Rusty nails or metal connectors
  • Daylight through roof decking

Why It Matters

Moisture can lead to rot, mold, insulation damage, and structural repairs.

Action

Trace stains upward, photograph the area, and schedule roof or flashing repair.

5 / 14High Risk
ST

Structure & Framing

Rafters, trusses, and connectorssplit raftermissing connector

Look For

  • Cracked, bowed, or sagging rafters
  • Cut, notched, or modified trusses
  • Loose plates, ties, nails, or hangers
  • Gaps, settlement, or uneven roof lines
  • Temporary or DIY repairs

Why It Matters

Structural defects can compromise roof safety and become expensive quickly.

Action

Do not disturb framing. Have a qualified contractor or engineer evaluate it.

6 / 14Medium Risk
VE

Ventilation

Vent opening and air channelblocked soffitair channel

Look For

  • Soffit vents blocked by insulation
  • Ridge, gable, or roof vents present
  • Baffles at soffits where needed
  • Extreme heat or stale air
  • Bath fans venting into attic

Why It Matters

Poor airflow raises energy costs and encourages mold, rot, and ice dams.

Action

Clear blocked intake paths and route exhaust ducts outdoors.

7 / 14Medium Risk
IN

Insulation

Insulation depth and coveragethin areastain

Look For

  • Depth appropriate for your climate
  • Even coverage across attic floor
  • Compressed or disturbed spots
  • Wet, stained, or moldy insulation
  • Gaps around lights, vents, and walls

Why It Matters

Weak insulation wastes energy and can hide moisture or pest problems.

Action

Air seal first, then restore depth with clean, dry insulation.

8 / 14High Risk
EL

Electrical

Attic wiring and junctionsopen boxoverheated wire

Look For

  • Exposed wiring or loose cables
  • Open junction boxes
  • Improper splices or connectors
  • Damaged or overheated wires
  • Recessed lights buried in insulation

Why It Matters

Electrical defects can cause shock, fire, and hidden overheating.

Action

Do not touch suspect wiring. Call a licensed electrician.

HVAC & Mechanical

Ductwork and mechanical runsloose ductcondensation

Look For

  • Ducts connected and sealed
  • Crushed, torn, or disconnected runs
  • Insulation on ducts
  • Condensation on equipment
  • Supported mechanical components

Why It Matters

Leaks waste energy, reduce comfort, and can create mold conditions.

Action

Seal duct leaks, support equipment, and route fans to the exterior.

10 / 14Medium Risk
PE

Pests & Rodents

Droppings, nesting, and entry signsdroppingsentry gap

Look For

  • Droppings or urine stains
  • Nests or shredded insulation
  • Chewed wires, wood, or duct liners
  • Insect wings, casings, or frass
  • Gaps at vents or roof edges

Why It Matters

Pests can damage wiring, contaminate insulation, and create entry paths.

Action

Seal entry points after removal and replace contaminated insulation.

FI

Fire & Heat Damage

Scorching and heat damagecharred woodhot fixture

Look For

  • Charred or blackened wood
  • Scorch marks near chimneys or flues
  • Overheated wiring or devices
  • Melted insulation or plastic
  • Combustibles too close to heat sources

Why It Matters

Heat damage may point to past fires or an active fire hazard.

Action

Stop inspection and call a pro if you see charring or overheating.

CH

Chimney & Exhaust

Chimney penetration and clearanceflashing leakclearance

Look For

  • Proper flashing around chimney
  • Cracked or deteriorated masonry
  • Gaps between chimney and framing
  • Soot stains or flue leaks
  • Vents that end inside the attic

Why It Matters

Bad clearances and leaks can cause fire risk, moisture, and exhaust issues.

Action

Keep combustibles clear and have chimney, roof, or vent defects repaired.

Safety & Access

Ladder, lighting, and access openingsecure laddersharp edge

Look For

  • Access opening large enough to enter
  • Ladder or stairs secure and stable
  • Walkways or platforms where needed
  • Exposed nails, screws, or sharp edges
  • Asbestos or vermiculite insulation

Why It Matters

Access problems create injury risk before the inspection even begins.

Action

Use proper lighting, stay on framing or platforms, and avoid suspect insulation.
