Safety & Access

stable ladderlighting

Look For

  • Secure hatch, ladder, or stairs
  • Walk boards or visible joists
  • Exposed nails, low headroom, loose debris
  • Vermiculite or suspect asbestos insulation

Why It Matters

Most attic injuries happen before the inspection even starts.

Action

Use PPE, strong lighting, and stop if footing, heat, wiring, or contamination feels unsafe.

Leaks & Moisture

stainingdrips

Look For

  • Stains on sheathing, rafters, or insulation
  • Damp wood, rusted nails, or wet insulation
  • Musty odor or mold-like growth
  • Daylight at roof decking or penetrations

Why It Matters

Moisture drives rot, mold, insulation failure, and hidden roof repairs.

Action

Trace stains upward and photograph the leak path before repairs hide the evidence.

4 / 12High Risk
ST

Structure

cut trusssagging

Look For

  • Cracked, split, bowed, or sagging rafters
  • Cut, drilled, or modified trusses
  • Missing connector plates or bracing
  • Temporary supports or odd DIY repairs

Why It Matters

Framing changes can reduce roof strength and affect the whole house.

Action

Do not alter framing. Refer suspicious structure to a qualified contractor or engineer.

5 / 12Medium Risk
VE

Ventilation

intakeexhaust

Look For

  • Blocked soffit vents
  • Missing or crushed baffles
  • Ridge, gable, or roof vents present
  • Bathroom or dryer fans dumping inside

Why It Matters

Bad airflow traps heat and moisture, raising bills and encouraging mold.

Action

Keep intake and exhaust paths clear, and route all exhaust outdoors.

6 / 12Medium Risk
IN

Insulation & Air Sealing

thin spotair leak

Look For

  • Low, uneven, compressed, or missing insulation
  • Dirty insulation showing air leakage
  • Gaps around pipes, wires, lights, and chases
  • Unsealed attic hatch or knee wall areas

Why It Matters

Air leaks and weak insulation waste energy and can move moisture into the attic.

Action

Air seal first, then add clean, dry insulation to the right depth for the climate.

7 / 12High Risk
EL

Electrical

open boxdamaged wire

Look For

  • Open junction boxes or exposed splices
  • Loose, damaged, or unsupported wiring
  • Old knob-and-tube or abandoned cables
  • Recessed lights buried in insulation

Why It Matters

Electrical defects can shock, overheat, or start a fire out of sight.

Action

Do not touch suspicious wiring. Photograph it and call a licensed electrician.

8 / 12Medium Risk
HV

HVAC & Exhaust

loose ductcondensation

Look For

  • Disconnected, crushed, or leaky ducts
  • Missing duct insulation
  • Condensation on ducts or equipment
  • Bath, kitchen, or dryer exhaust ending in attic

Why It Matters

Mechanical leaks waste energy and can dump moisture into hidden spaces.

Action

Seal ducts, support equipment, insulate cold surfaces, and vent exhaust outside.

9 / 12Medium Risk
PL

Plumbing, Chimneys & Flues

pipe bootclearance

Look For

  • Leaks near vent pipes and roof penetrations
  • Cracked chimney masonry or soot stains
  • Gaps around framing and flues
  • Combustibles too close to hot surfaces

Why It Matters

These penetrations are common leak points and can also create fire or exhaust hazards.

Action

Keep clearances visible and repair flashing, masonry, or disconnected vents promptly.

10 / 12Medium Risk
PE

Pests

droppingsentry gap

Look For

  • Droppings, urine stains, trails, or nesting
  • Chewed wires, ducts, insulation, or wood
  • Damaged vent screens or roof edge gaps
  • Dead insects, frass, wings, or casings

Why It Matters

Pests contaminate insulation, damage systems, and often reveal exterior openings.

Action

Remove pests first, seal entry points second, then replace contaminated materials.

11 / 12High Risk
FI

Fire & Heat Damage

charhot fixture

Look For

  • Charred or blackened framing
  • Scorch marks near chimneys, lights, or wiring
  • Melted plastic or insulation
  • Combustible storage near heat sources

Why It Matters

Heat damage may be evidence of an old event or an active hazard.

Action

Stop the inspection for active burn smells, charring, or overheated wiring.
